Discover the latest trends, tips, and inspirations tailored for the Indian fashionista.

1. Master the Art of Saree Draping

Tip:
Learn different draping styles like Nivi, Bengali, and Gujarati to suit various occasions. The Nivi drape, is the most common and versatile, perfect for both casual and formal occasions. The Bengali drape, is ideal for festive events and cultural celebrations. The Gujarati drape is perfect for traditional gatherings and family functions.


Trick:
Use safety pins to secure pleats and ensure your saree stays in place all day. For a modern touch, drape your saree in a contemporary way, such as pairing it with a belt or draping it over pants.

2. Choosing the Perfect Saree Fabric for Every Occasion

Tip:
Opt for silk sarees for weddings and special events, cotton for everyday wear, and chiffon or georgette for parties.Silk sarees, with their rich textures and intricate designs, are ideal for weddings and special occasions.


Trick:
To keep silk sarees looking pristine, store them in muslin cloth to allow the fabric to breathe and avoid moisture damage. When wearing chiffon or georgette sarees, use a petticoat that matches your skin tone for a seamless look. For cotton sarees, starch them lightly to maintain their crispness and avoid excessive wrinkles.

3. Elevate Your Lehenga Game

Tip:
Select lehengas based on your body type.Choosing the right lehenga style can enhance your natural shape and ensure you look your best. A-line lehengas, which flare out from the waist to form an 'A' shape, create a balanced silhouette suitable for most body types. Fish-cut lehengas, which hug the body till the knees and then flare out, accentuate curves and add a touch of glamour, making them ideal for hourglass figures.


Trick:
Use a waist belt to adjust the length of your lehenga if it's too long, without needing alterations. This also helps define your waist and adds a stylish accessory.

4. Incorporate Ethnic Jackets

Tip:
Add an ethnic jacket to your jeans and top or over a dress for a chic Indo-Western look.


Trick:
Pin the jacket with a brooch to experiment with different styles. This not only secures the jacket but also adds a decorative element to your outfit. Choose brooches that complement the jacket's color and style, or opt for statement pieces that contrast for a bold fashion statement. Additionally, you can use multiple brooches to create a unique pattern or cluster effect, enhancing the overall aesthetic appeal of your ensemble.

5. Pair Kurtis with Jeans

Tip:
Wear long kurtis with skinny jeans for a balanced look and short kurtis with flared jeans for a retro vibe.


Trick:
Pin the jacket with a brooch to experiment with different styles. This not only secures the jacket but also adds a decorative element to your outfit. Choose brooches that complement the jacket's color and style, or opt for statement pieces that contrast for a bold fashion statement.

6. Dress Smart for Monsoons

Tip:
Choose water-resistant fabrics like nylon and polyester, and avoid long dresses or pants that can get wet easily. Nylon and polyester are excellent choices as they are lightweight, durable, and quick-drying, making them ideal for unpredictable weather conditions.


Trick:
Keep a small, foldable raincoat or poncho in your bag for unexpected showers. Opt for bright colors or fun prints to make a statement even on rainy days.

7. Accessorize Thoughtfully

Tip:
Accessories play a crucial role in completing your look, enhancing the overall aesthetic of your outfit. When wearing simple, solid-colored outfits, statement jewelry pieces such as chunky necklaces or bold earrings can add a touch of glamour and serve as focal points. These pieces create a striking contrast and draw attention to your ensemble


Trick:
Statement pieces like bold earrings or a statement necklace can elevate even the simplest outfits. Choose one focal point to avoid overwhelming your look
